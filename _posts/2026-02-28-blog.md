## Y2 • Week 28
Caught up for lunch this week with a friend in Lamalou-les-Bains, well known for restorative thermal spas. Tradition has it that a peasant suffering from pain bathed here and received considerable relief from his ailment… not sure exactly when but roughly 12th century. Daily buses and ambulances from all over the southeast transport patients needing restoring. Also set in the 12th century, I’m enjoying a series (listening) by Blanche d'Alpuget (Bob Hawke’s second wife) about the birth of the House of Plantagenet focusing on Henry II and his union with Eleanor of Aquitaine, who was Queen of France and subsequently Queen of England.

With a group of mad rugby supporters I rugged up and went to watch Béziers team playing a home game last night… probably won’t repeat the exercise, but it was good to see inside the stadium which is very prominent on the landscape.

France has again ranked as the world’s most visited country -- 102 million international tourists in 2025 – a dozen or so who came to visit me 😀.
