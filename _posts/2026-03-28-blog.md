## Y2 • Week 32
Throughout France 4500 sirens can be heard for a minute at midday on the first Wednesday of every month • a sound test carrying on since WWII. In the event of a real incident (industrial accident, severe flood or dam failure) the sound varies and is not continuous. I’ve looked up where shelter locations are situated just in case the incident is war. 🤣

It’s turned nippy again – 4° in Paris last week when I ducked out early for a baguette. Back home this week the skies are blue and the wind relentless – and an electric blanket continues to keep me alive at night.

In Paris we came across Wallace Fountains – installed throughout Paris 1850(ish) by a wealthy aristocrat Sir Richard Wallace. Over 100 are still in existence today and continue to provide drinking water for the public. On the way home from Paris we stopped for quick breaks in Fontainebleau (opulent palace) and Nevers (nice bridge).
